The Crisis: Critical Housing Shortage in Vanderhoof

Adults with developmental disabilities face a significant shortage of affordable, accessible, and supportive housing. We must move now to protect our most vulnerable neighbors.

“On the contrary, those parts of the body that seem to be weaker are indispensable, and the parts that we think are less honorable we treat with special honor.” — 1 Corinthians 12:22–23 (NIV)

Why This Matters Now

The City of Vanderhoof urgently needs safe, supportive homes for adults with developmental disabilities. Without further investment, adults with developmental disabilities will continue to face displacement, isolation and unsafe living situations. Charis Supportive Housing Society exists with the purpose of seeking to ensure that every person experience the dignity of a home, access to care, and the community that embraces them for the long term.

Affordability Gap

Residents can afford about $500/month, but local rents are more than double.

Access Gap

Over half of local homes are older and inaccessible for mobility needs.

Caregiver Stress

Aging parents ask, "What happens when I can't do this anymore?"

Project Snapshot

Designed for Support

Duplex layout enables shared living as well as 24/7 on-site support staff to assist with daily life skills, health, and connection.

Dedicated Housing

Reserved exclusively for adults who have developmental disabilities requiring assisted living, stability, and accessibility.

Immediate Impact

Developing this project now prevents displacement, relieves caregiver stress, and creates a permanent supportive option in Vanderhoof.

The Opportunity

Charis Supportive Housing Society is partnering with Nechako View Senior Citizens' Home Society as they move toward the purchase of a purpose-driven duplex in Vanderhoof.

Once the acquisition is finalized, Charis will lease the home from Nechako View and lead the renovation work needed to create a safe, dignified, and supportive living environment for local residents who require consistent support.

The layout already suits shared living with on-site staff, meaning donor support for renovations and upgrades will translate into safe housing almost immediately.

What We Are Facing

Adults with developmental disabilities deserve a permanent home, not a patchwork of temporary solutions. The Connaught Duplex keeps residents close to family, services, and community.
